BC Lightstation Sea-Surface Temperature

From Cummins & Masson (2014): "Surface water temperature and salinity have been measured daily at several BC lighthouse stations for many decades. [...] Water properties are measured from a bucket sample drawn from the top meter of the water column within an hour of local daytime high tide. Due to changes in instrumentation and measurement technique, the nominal uncertainty of the daily temperature and salinity observations has varied over time from 0.3 to 0.1 °C and 0.03 to 0.3 psu, respectively (Peter Chandler, pers. comm.). The uncertainty on monthly averaged temperature and salinity data should be no greater than 0.1 °C and 0.1 psu, respectively."

The BC Lightstation sea-surface temperature data used here, as well as sea-surface salinity data, can be found on Open Data.

Notes

  • Any one-month gaps were filled with spline interpolation to get continuous records.
  • For the Langara Island record, the analysis period for the 95% confidence interval starts in Mar. 1940 to avoid the 30-month data gap (Sept. 1937 - Feb. 1940) near the beginning of the record. The least-squares trend for the abridged record is 0.88 °C/century compared with 0.86 °C/century for the entire record.
  • For the Entrance Island record, the analysis period for the 95% confidence interval is limited to May 1936 - Dec. 2019 to avoid interpolating over the extended data gaps occurring near the end of the record. The least-squares trend for the abridged record is 1.28 °C/century compared with 1.29 °C/century for the entire record.
